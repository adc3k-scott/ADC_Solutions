## A. Robotaxi status (verified 2026-06-12)

- **Unsupervised commercial robotaxi service is live in Dallas and Houston since
  2026-04-18** — the first fully unsupervised deployments, expanded from the
  original supervised Austin service.
- **Seven-city target by mid-2026:** Dallas, Houston (live); Phoenix, Miami,
  Orlando, Tampa, Las Vegas (targeted before mid-2026). Fleet size on the order
  of ~1,000 vehicles US-wide in 2026 (Morgan Stanley estimate).
- **Fleet charging model:** Tesla runs charging/cleaning/maintenance through its
  existing footprint — the ~80,000-stall Supercharger network (+2,500 stalls in
  Q1 2026 alone), service centers, and delivery locations — and has said publicly
  that **more capacity must be added as the service expands**.

### The Louisiana gap — opportunity framing for EVH-HUB-001

Tesla has **no announced robotaxi presence in Louisiana**. The expansion list is
Texas, Arizona, Nevada, Florida — I-10 both directions, skipping the Gulf middle.
When the corridor fills in, an operator needs depot-scale charging, cleaning, and
staging real estate in Lafayette — exactly what EVH-HUB-001 evaluates at 1016 SW
Evangeline Thruway (already designed as a charging station, decision 0014 §4).

Framing discipline: this is **ADC's candidate-site thesis, not a Tesla plan** —
no forward document may assert Tesla intends Louisiana operations. The
EVH-HUB-001 non-Tesla fallback (fleet/depot charging for any operator — including
non-Tesla AV fleets, logistics EVs, or transit) carries the concept on its own.
Controls boundary per decision 0014 §2 is unaffected: a charging hub is a
site-host/power play; Tesla software never enters ADC nodes.

## B. The Tesla-solar §48E question (CAP-OI-08 input)

Scott directed 2026-06-12 [W]: evaluate Tesla against First Solar for the §48E
qualifying module order (begin-construction cutoff 2026-07-04, CAP-OI-07).
What the record shows going in:

1. **What Tesla actually sells in solar:** Solar Roof (residential shingle
   product) and retrofit panel installations. Tesla has historically sourced
   third-party modules (e.g., Qcells) for retrofit work; its Buffalo Gigafactory
   output centers on Solar Roof tiles. **Tesla does not field a
   commercial/utility-scale module product line** comparable to First Solar's
   Series 6/7.
2. **Domestic content:** First Solar's Series 7 (Ohio/Alabama/Louisiana-adjacent
   supply chain; New Iberia facility ~30 mi from Lafayette) is the
   domestic-content benchmark and the basis on which it was named the §48E
   qualifying instrument. Any Tesla offer must match that test to be eligible
   on equal terms.
3. **What would change the answer:** a written Tesla commercial-scale offer with
   (a) module supply meeting domestic-content bonus rules, (b) deliverable
   against a 2026-07-04 begin-construction order, (c) commercial rooftop
   (±173 kSF Evangeline / Trappey's 2.05 MW) suitability. Absent all three in
   hand by the **2026-06-19 cutoff** (decision 0016 §4), the First Solar order
   proceeds with zero further delay.
4. **Where Tesla fits regardless of the §48E outcome:** charging-hub canopy
   solar, post-deadline phases, and Megapack-class storage in the colo/CSP
   energy-shifting lane (decision 0014 §3) — all under the same
   vendor-not-partner, Attachment-A-gated rules.
