## A. Why "AI data centers in space" matters to ADC

This is the single most ADC-relevant sentence to come out of the IPO. Three readings,
all favorable:

1. **Orbital compute has a ground segment.** Every orbital data-center architecture
   still needs ground stations, gateway power, downlink processing sites, and
   terrestrial staging/integration facilities. 100,000+ satellites multiplies the
   Starlink gateway buildout that decision 0013 §3 already names as ADC's winnable
   near-term lane (~$500K–$2M/site in gensets + BESS + power conditioning).
2. **The hard problem in orbital compute is thermal.** In vacuum there is no
   convection; every watt must be pumped to radiators by liquid loops. Liquid-cooling
   distribution — CDU/TCS-class engineering — is exactly the competence ADC has
   spec'd to OCP standards. ADC will not build flight hardware, but the supplier
   ecosystem for development, test, and ground-qualification rigs is terrestrial.
3. **Terrestrial AI buildout funds the transition.** The xAI/Colossus segment
   (gas generation + grid batteries, behind-the-meter) is the proven pattern ADC's
   10 MW node product replicates at modular scale (decision 0013 context). IPO
   proceeds accelerate that buildout before any orbital system exists.

Forward use: this hook goes into pitch letter Rev 0.3 (one paragraph, truthful
two-lane framing preserved). It does not change any topology decision — Lane B
remains 0011-watch-gated.
