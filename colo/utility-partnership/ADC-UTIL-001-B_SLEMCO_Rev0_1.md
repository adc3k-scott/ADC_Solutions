## 1. The SLEMCO frame (everything bends to these three facts)

1. **Distribution-only, no generation:** ~119,000 meters across nine
   Acadiana parishes; ~10,500+ miles of line and **37 substations**;
   ~$304M FY2024 revenue; ~2.6M MWh retail sales.
2. **Full-requirements contract:** ~9.75-year contract with NextEra
   Energy Marketing supplies essentially all power. Anything that
   displaces NextEra purchases or exports to the grid triggers
   wholesale-contract, LPSC, and 300-foot-rule complications SLEMCO
   will reflexively resist.
3. **Culture:** financially conservative, engineering-led,
   member-obsessed, "least-cost provider in the State of Louisiana,"
   no base-rate increase 2015→2025. Decision circle is short: CEO/GM
   Katherine Domingue, Dir. Engineering & Strategic Planning Jim
   Laque, VP Ops David Moore, CFO Nicole Clements, 10-member
   parish-based board (pres. David Simon).

**Therefore the pitch is the proven PROP-002 frame:** the data center
becomes a **new SLEMCO retail member whose load is additive** while
the BTM node supplies the compute behind the meter —
**contract-neutral** to the NextEra arrangement. SLEMCO's
commercial/industrial rates (~28% below national average) make the
retail relationship genuinely attractive for ADC's house/aux load.

## 2. Program elements, SLEMCO-cut

| Element | SLEMCO instance |
|---|---|
| 10 MW block | BTM at a member site (Trappey's-class brownfield or suburban-ring industrial parcel in SLEMCO territory); **no export, no displacement** — additive member load only |
| Drone inspection | 37 substations and 10,500+ line-miles maintained by a lean co-op crew is the strongest inspection case of the three utilities; a dock at the partnership site covers nearby SLEMCO substations/feeders, dispatch-priority on storm restoration |
| Telemetry grid services | Scoped to what a full-requirements co-op can accept: monitoring, storm-restoration coordination, and member-side resilience first; market-facing dispatch only if/where contract-compatible [O — working session] |
| Federal alignment | Co-op-specific rows: **USDA New ERA** (~90% obligated; watch de-obligations/realignment) and **PACE** ($1B partially forgivable loans — active); **40101(d) via LDENR** round-2 RFPs (microgrids/critical infrastructure); GRIP future rounds |

**Timing note [W]:** no hyperscale data center sits in SLEMCO
territory today while Louisiana's data-center boom concentrates
elsewhere (Meta Richland Parish, Amazon Caddo/Bossier, Applied
Digital Rapides). First-mover member-load-additive compute is an
unclaimed position for SLEMCO — and ADC's program is sized in 10 MW
blocks, not gigawatts, which fits a co-op's risk culture.
