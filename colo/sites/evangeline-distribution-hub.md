## The economics (comparison memo bottom line)

- Greenfield replacement: **$19.4M–$26.6M all-in** ($112–153/SF);
  ex-land $18.4M–$24.8M. Asking $6M ≈ $34.59/SF — far below
  replacement cost, **but not fee-simple**: the ground lease is the
  entire question.
- Quoted $5/SF/yr NNN is materially below replacement-cost rent
  ($8.96–13.80/SF/yr at 8–9 % yield-on-cost).
- Existing asset saves an estimated 12–24+ months vs greenfield.
- Assessed (2025): total $801,070; taxes ≈ $1,485 city + $6,742 parish.

## Key diligence (memo §5, condensed)

1. **Ground-lease economics** — term, rent, escalations, renewals,
   assignment, lender protections, reversion of improvements. This is
   the deal.
2. Building condition (roof, slab, dock, sprinkler, electrical, former
   cold-storage penetrations).
3. Civil/site, environmental (Phase I; railroad-adjacent history —
   landowner IS the railroad), operational fit.
4. Power demand fit — memo treats it as a standard dry warehouse; any
   compute/manufacturing electrification need is unmodeled [O].
