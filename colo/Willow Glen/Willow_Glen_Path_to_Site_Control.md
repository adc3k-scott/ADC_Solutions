## Objective

Move from no position to a locked, capital-efficient site-control
instrument on the Willow Glen Terminal (WGT) campus footprint — without
an outright purchase — while protecting the originator position and
holding the one input the landowner cannot replicate.

## Guiding principles

- **Control, not ownership.** The instrument is a long-term ground
  lease (WGT retains title) and/or a purchase option — not a $175M cash
  acquisition. This preserves capital for interconnection, generation,
  and build.
- **Assume a sophisticated, informed counterparty.** WGT is held by
  Zydeco / Willow Glen Ventures; Yorktown is an energy PE firm. The
  River Bend comp 25 miles north has already made the monetization
  model public. Do not premise protection on the owner failing to
  understand the value — they will understand it the moment it is
  articulated.
- **An NDA is not protection by itself.** It restrains disclosure to
  third parties; it does not stop the owner from acting on the thesis
  themselves. Originator protection comes from (a) a non-circumvention
  / non-solicitation clause, (b) a signed exclusivity / right of first
  negotiation, and (c) control of a scarce input.
- **Hold the scarce input.** The one thing that is yours and not WGT's
  is confirmed Entergy/MISO load-serving capacity. Secure that inquiry
  independently and early.
- **Stage the reveal.** Lead with a teaser. Release the full economics
  and the monetization roadmap only after the NDA + non-circ is signed.

## Phase 0 — Before contact: protect + arm (parallel, ~Weeks 0–3)

- Engage transactional real estate / energy counsel to paper: a mutual
  NDA with a non-circumvention clause, and a template exclusivity /
  right-of-first-negotiation for when talks turn serious. (Counsel
  finalizes structure; this plan is not legal advice.)
- File the Entergy large-load interconnection inquiry for the Willow
  Glen site — independent of WGT. Goal: a written, quantified
  servable-MW figure and the MISO study path. This is the moat and the
  single biggest diligence item in the deal.
- Prepare a two-tier document set:
  - **Tier 1 — Teaser (1–2 pages):** the opportunity in outline —
    power-ready brownfield, ground-lease structure, WGT keeps title,
    capital-backed — with no full economics and no monetization
    playbook. This is what opens the door.
  - **Tier 2 — Full proposal (the revised deck):** released only
    post-NDA. Fix the two credibility items first: reframe "450 MW
    already wired / nothing to confirm" as "interconnect head start, MW
    subject to Entergy/MISO study," and rebuild the timeline to
    something a reader who knows River Bend will believe.
- Confirm the capital partner (ORNN). Have the financing relationship
  far enough along that you can represent credible, committed capital
  at reveal — so you present as a sponsor with a team, not a sole
  originator handing over an idea.

## Phase 1 — First contact + NDA (~Weeks 3–5)

- Direct outreach to WGT principals (GM and the Yorktown / Zydeco
  capital side).
- Lead with the Tier 1 teaser: a structured, capital-backed proposal to
  monetize the asset stack via a long-term ground lease with revenue
  participation, WGT retains title — specifics to follow under NDA.
- Execute the mutual NDA + non-circ before releasing Tier 2.

Outcome: NDA signed; principal-level meeting scheduled.

## Phase 2 — Structured proposal + exclusivity (~Weeks 5–9)

- Present the full (corrected) proposal under NDA, paired with the
  credibility you've assembled — the ORNN capital relationship and the
  Entergy interconnection data.
- Ask for 60–90 days of exclusivity / right of first negotiation,
  during which WGT pauses marketing the campus footprint, in exchange
  for committed diligence resources and (recommended) a modest
  non-refundable exclusivity deposit to bind seriousness.
- This is the true site-control inflection: exclusivity is what stops
  them shopping it.

Outcome: signed exclusivity / ROFN.

## Phase 3 — LOI on commercial terms (~Weeks 9–13)

- Non-binding term sheet covering: lease term, base ground rent,
  escalator, revenue-participation structure,
  retained-terminal-business carve-out, and milestones / conditions
  precedent — interconnection confirmation, capital close, anchor-tenant
  LOI.
- Make interconnection a condition precedent so you are not committed
  before the gate clears.

Outcome: executed LOI.

## Phase 4 — Diligence + definitive (~Weeks 13–24)

Run in parallel:

- Title + ALTA survey
- Substation walkdown + Entergy/MISO load-study confirmation (the gate)
- River-cooling permit confirmation
- Phase I/II environmental — retired power station; screen for legacy
  oil / PCB / asbestos
- Structural / geometric fit study on existing pads and switchgear
  foundations (reuse vs. demo/recast)
- Parish PILOT / ITEP pre-engagement (file ITEP before construction)

Execute the definitive ground lease (or exercise the option). Site
control achieved.

## What makes or breaks it

- **Interconnection is the gate.** Confirm servable MW before
  definitive. Everything downstream is contingent on it.
- **Protection ≠ secrecy.** Your defense is signed exclusivity +
  non-circ + a scarce input you hold + speed — not the owner's
  ignorance.
- **Control is necessary, not sufficient.** You still need committed
  capital (ORNN) and an anchor tenant / offtake to finance the build.
  Structure the lease with milestones so you are not exposed to
  multibillion-dollar construction before the deal is real.
- **Speed compounds.** Unhomed Anthropic Tranche-3 capacity and active
  hyperscaler site-walking in the corridor mean the option decays each
  quarter. Move — but move protected.
