## Concept

The building was already designed with a charging-station use in mind
(Scott 2026-06-12 [W]; charging imagery in this site folder). The
concept: position part of the Evangeline footprint as a
**vehicle-charging / robotaxi staging hub** — Tesla site-host model or
equivalent — alongside whatever BL1 use COLO-04 selects, monetizing
yard + power infrastructure ADC would build anyway.

Why it fits ADC:
- **Power is the product.** A charging hub is a behind-the-meter power
  problem — utility feed, transformation, DC fast-charge plant,
  storage buffering — squarely inside ADC's BL1 competence and
  potentially served by ADC's own microgrid/BESS hardware.
- **Lane B competence builder (ADC-LANEB-001):** EV charging is the
  one domain where 400/800 V-class DC components ship today. Building
  a DC charging plant exercises the exact component classes (fuses,
  contactors, connectors, ground-fault monitoring) Lane B needs, with
  none of the pre-production risk.
- **Solar overlay (decision 0009 §4):** the ~173 kSF roof is already
  in the §48E solar scope conversation (CAP-OI-07); charging load is
  a natural offtaker for rooftop generation + storage.

## Constraints and gating diligence (in order)

1. **Union Pacific ground lease.** Any site-host arrangement, charging
   easement, or third-party occupancy needs the UP ground-lease
   abstract first (EVA-OI-01 lineage) — landlord consent provisions,
   permitted-use clauses, assignment/sublease rights. **This gates
   everything.**
2. **Lease execution status** (CAP-OI-02) — concept work cannot
   precede ADC's own occupancy rights; dual-agency disclosure with
   Mark Johns / Elifin stays in writing.
3. **Utility service capacity** at the site for multi-MW charging
   load (LUS/Entergy territory check per CAM-OI-03 pattern) — a
   charging hub may also be a microgrid-node customer, which is the
   better story.
4. **Counterparty reality check:** Tesla builds and owns most of its
   charging infrastructure; third-party hosting exists but on Tesla's
   terms. The robotaxi network's Louisiana timeline is unknown — no
   ADC document asserts a Tesla commitment, timeline, or program
   eligibility as fact. The concept must also price a non-Tesla
   fallback (fleet/depot charging for any operator, including
   oilfield-service fleets per the OFS program).

## What this memo is not

No capital commitment, no topology change, no COLO-04 ruling, no
external outreach. Outreach to Tesla or any charging counterparty is
an outward-facing action requiring its own decision record per repo
convention.
