Architecture

10 MW Firm Technical Node

Slide 04 of 12

Cat G3520H Gensets

Using 2.5 MW-class lean-burn natural-gas engines; final continuous output must account for site conditions and fuel quality.

N+1 Standby Core

Using 5 gensets per 10 MW node: 4 online for firm output, with 1 standby/maintenance unit for N+1 resilience.

Step-Up Scheme

Dedicated MV transformer scheme to match selected generator voltage to the confirmed Entergy POI bus voltage.

Modular Scale

Replicable standardized layout enables scale-up planning to 20 modules (200 MW), subject to land, gas, emissions, noise, and interconnection limits.

Technical Standardization

By standardizing on packaged 10 MW building blocks, we simplify repeatable electrical and civil design while leaving site-specific interconnection, gas, emissions, and noise work as critical path items.

Grid Dynamics

Dynamic Response & LFP Storage

Slide 05 of 12

Solving the AI Duty-Cycle Problem

AI Training profiles exhibit sudden step-changes in electrical demand. Gas gensets can struggle with instant ramp commands, causing voltage/frequency sag.

Our proposed 6 MW / 3 MWh LFP BESS serves as a high-speed dynamic buffer, smoothing step transients while the gas engines ramp over normal governor and fuel-air response cycles.

Regulatory

Permitting & Emissions Strategy

Slide 06 of 12

Synthetic-Minor Framework

Total facility potential-to-emit would need enforceable operational and/or mechanical limits below applicable major-source thresholds to pursue a synthetic-minor path.

Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR)

Post-combustion SCR with urea or ammonia reagent can target >90% NOx reduction; final limits depend on engine certification, stack testing, and permit conditions.

Oxidation Catalysts

Oxidation catalysts reduce CO and formaldehyde; compliance must be demonstrated against 40 CFR 60 Subpart JJJJ and applicable permit limits.

The Attainment Path

EPA Green Book data should be cited as showing no current active nonattainment listing for Lafayette Parish under current criteria pollutants; final applicability still depends on LDEQ and EPA review.

Use a 90-to-120 day minor-source review period only as a planning target after LDEQ pre-application alignment, application completeness, modeling, and public-notice requirements are confirmed.
