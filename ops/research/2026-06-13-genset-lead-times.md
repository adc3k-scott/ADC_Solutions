# Research — NG genset lead times & the acquisition-fund window (2026-06-13)

Supports [decision 0017](../decisions/0017-ng-genset-acquisition-fund.md) and
[ADC-FUND-001](../../company/capital/ADC-FUND-001_genset-btm-acquisition-fund_Rev0_1.md).
Verified the "scarcity window" thesis before it goes in front of Ornn.

## Findings (sourced — do not assert beyond these)

- **Generator lead times exceed two years, in some cases approaching three.**
  US data-center equipment lead times have stretched to ~42 weeks on
  average (≈83% above the 2019 baseline); gensets sit at the long end.
- **Large-frame natural-gas turbines are reported sold out through 2028.**
  Developers are pivoting to reciprocating gas engines, which rival
  turbines in manufacturing scale and deploy faster (xAI stood up a data
  center on recip engines in months).
- **RICE is the less-constrained class.** The worst bottlenecks are
  heavy-duty turbines; aeroderivatives, IGTs, and RICE systems are less
  constrained — which is exactly why recips are the attractive path for
  fast data-center prime power. (Supports ADC's reciprocating-engine node
  standard, MGN-BOD-002.)
- **Behind-the-meter prime power is being specified, not just standby.**
  Wärtsilä's 34SG debuted in a 412 MW US data-center project (April 2026);
  INNIO announced its largest-ever order — a 2.3 GW project with VoltaGrid
  (92 power packs × 25 MW). Recip gas engines are now primary BTM power.
- **The driver is grid bypass.** Data centers are turning to on-site gas
  for prime power specifically to eliminate multi-year grid-interconnection
  lead times — the moat ADC-FUND-001 §2 names.

## So what (for the fund)

The thesis holds and is corroborated by the broader market, not just ADC's
read: new iron is on multi-year allocation, recips are the fast path, and
BTM prime power is mainstream. ADC's differentiation is **not** "use gas"
(everyone is) — it's (1) buying the *available global secondary-market*
inventory now via a pooled vehicle, (2) the 50 Hz/60 Hz sourcing edge for
islanded compute loads, and (3) Louisiana gas. The crowd validates the
lane; the fund's edge is sourcing speed + frequency reach + fuel.

## Caveats / open

- Numbers are directional from trade press; **OEM-specific quotes are not
  in hand.** Before stating a specific lead time to Ornn, confirm against a
  live quote or hold the claim at "multi-year, industry-reported."
- That xAI / VoltaGrid / Wärtsilä projects bought *new* capacity at scale
  also means the secondary market is being actively drained — reinforces
  "time is the thesis," but also means ADC is competing for the same iron.
