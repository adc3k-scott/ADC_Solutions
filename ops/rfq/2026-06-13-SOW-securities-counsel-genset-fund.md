## 1. Background

ADC Solutions Inc (Louisiana; Scott Tomsu, President) manufactures
modular AI-datacenter power and cooling infrastructure and is standing up
a **pooled vehicle to acquire ≥1 MW natural-gas gensets** (50 Hz and
60 Hz) on the global secondary market for behind-the-meter (BTM) compute.
Outside participants would **co-own the acquired assets**; ADC would serve
as **manager/sponsor**, earning a development fee plus carried interest.
The first deployment is ADC's own-use BTM compute site on the company's
Louisiana property triangle (decision 0009). Preferred structure under
decision 0017 is a **Delaware series LLC** ring-fencing each deployment
node, with ADC as managing member — subject to counsel's review.

The economics are time-sensitive (new gensets are on multi-year
allocation; available secondary-market inventory is finite — see
[ops/research/2026-06-13-genset-lead-times.md](../research/2026-06-13-genset-lead-times.md)),
so ADC needs the **fastest compliant path to a first close**, not a
maximal structure. Speed-to-compliant-close is the optimization target.

## 2. Scope — six deliverables, one engagement

### D1 — Entity & ownership structure
- Recommend the holding structure: **Delaware series LLC vs LP vs parallel
  SPV-per-node**, weighing node ring-fencing (decision 0017 §2), sponsor
  control, investor familiarity, and formation/maintenance cost.
- Address the **co-ownership model specifically:** does "participants own
  the assets" mean LLC/LP membership interests, or direct
  tenancy-in-common (TIC) in the iron? Flag the distinct securities and
  tax consequences of TIC (each TIC interest can itself be a security;
  partition rights; management agreement) vs pooled-entity interests, and
  recommend.
- Sponsor entity: should ADC Solutions Inc be the manager directly, or a
  separate manager/GP entity it owns? Liability and fee-flow implications.
- Manager fiduciary duties, indemnification, and the operating/LP
  agreement waterfall (dev fee + carry).

### D2 — Offering exemption & investor eligibility
- Recommend the **Securities Act exemption:** Reg D **506(b)** (no general
  solicitation, up to 35 non-accredited with disclosure) vs **506(c)**
  (general solicitation permitted, **all investors accredited + verified**)
  vs Reg A vs an intrastate/Louisiana exemption. Trade-off: does ADC want
  to market publicly (favors 506(c)) or stay private to known relationships
  such as Ornn (favors 506(b))?
- **Accredited-investor verification** mechanics and who bears that step.
- Maximum investor count, integration risk across multiple node series,
  and bad-actor (Rule 506(d)) diligence on ADC principals.
- **Form D** federal filing and the trigger timing.

### D3 — Investment Company Act (1940) & Investment Advisers Act analysis
- Does the vehicle risk being an **"investment company"** under the '40
  Act, or does ownership/operation of gensets as productive assets keep it
  an **operating company** outside the Act? If pooling triggers it,
  identify the applicable exclusion (e.g., **3(c)(1)/3(c)(5)** or
  operating-company status) and the conditions to hold it.
- Does ADC, as manager, become an **"investment adviser"** requiring
  state or SEC registration, or does the **private-fund adviser exemption**
  apply? State (Louisiana) adviser-registration posture.

### D4 — Offering documents
- **Private Placement Memorandum (PPM)** with risk factors — including the
  ADC-specific risks already named in decision 0017/ADC-FUND-001:
  used/relocated-equipment condition, **prime-power air-permit
  qualification** (40 CFR 60 JJJJ / 63 ZZZZ), 50 Hz balance-of-plant
  matching, fuel-supply dependency, and the sponsor's own-use first
  deployment (affiliate-transaction / conflict disclosure).
- **Subscription agreement** + investor questionnaire.
- **Operating/LP agreement** implementing the D1 structure and the fee/carry
  waterfall.

### D5 — Blue-sky & ongoing compliance
- State (blue-sky) notice filings for Louisiana and each investor's
  state; fee schedule and timing tied to the D2 exemption.
- Ongoing obligations: investor reporting, **affiliate/conflict-of-interest
  governance** (ADC is both sponsor and the first customer), and amendment
  procedure as new node series are added.

### D6 — Adjacent-risk flag-and-refer (scope boundary)
Not for full work product — a **one-page issue-spot** identifying which of
the following need separate specialist counsel, so ADC engages them in
parallel rather than discovering them late:
- **Cross-border equipment acquisition:** OFAC/sanctions screening,
  export/import, and FCPA exposure when buying gensets overseas.
- **ERISA plan-asset** rules if any investor is a retirement plan.
- **Tax counsel** coordination on carried-interest treatment, UBTI for
  any tax-exempt investor, and the TIC-vs-entity choice from D1.
