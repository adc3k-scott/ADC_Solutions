## The one line

Scott Tomsu doesn't just *operate* complex systems — he **designs the hardest
part of the system and ships it.** For 25 years he has built, powered, rigged,
and run mission-critical electromechanical systems where failure is expensive
and no one is coming to help: subsea robotics to 3,000 meters, high-voltage and
fiber-optic power chains, national-championship race engines he designed himself,
and Louisiana construction he permits and self-performs. He was also **AI-native
ahead of his peers** — he built a working LLM voice agent and stood up a company
around it in early 2026, and today runs his business through autonomous AI.

The thread isn't a long résumé of unrelated things. It's **one discipline** —
*design it, build it, tune it to the limit, run it live* — applied to engines,
robots, power, structures, and now AI. An umbilical, a race motor, a launch-site
power spine, and an AI factory are the same problem: feed → transform →
distribute → signal over fiber → hold uptime in a hostile environment.

## The story

A self-made arc — every credential below was earned the hard way, none of it
handed down.

- **Born Omaha, Nebraska.** Rough start: bad neighborhood, failed the 7th grade.
  His mother moved them to rural **Georgia** to change the trajectory. All he had
  was a drum kit — he's a **drummer to this day**, and it's where he first learned
  to find the rhythm in a system.
- **Started working at 14.** Cutting grass → painting apartments → building houses
  with the people who owned them. **Parker Construction**, after school, every time
  he could catch a ride out of the middle of nowhere. He never planned on college —
  he was already moving up. He was promised the business; the owner went bankrupt
  and knocked him back to framing houses.
- **1996 — commercial dive school**, Divers Academy of the Eastern Seaboard,
  Camden NJ. Dead broke: no car, rode a **bicycle** to and from where he was
  staying. Then to **Louisiana** to start his diving career — and on to **ROV**
  (remotely operated vehicle) work, including the **RACAL → Thales Geosolutions**
  lineage.
- **2003** — when his daughter was born and Oceaneering bought out Thales, rather
  than switch companies for a pay cut he **pivoted to homebuilding**. Over the next
  several years he built **~80 houses** across Broussard, Youngsville, and New
  Iberia, plus the **Family Practice Medical Center in New Iberia** and the full
  interior of **Sakatini sushi restaurant** in Youngsville (still standing).
- **2009–2010** — out-of-state firms flooded in with cheap labor and bulk
  out-of-state materials and drove prices below what he could match. He exited.
- **2013 — back offshore to West Africa** with **Subsea 7** (out of Aberdeen,
  Scotland), on contract through **Compass** (Texas). **ROV Superintendent on MEGI
  (Mobil Equatorial Guinea Inc, ExxonMobil)**, running the **Pacific Wizard**
  (2013–2018) until roughly six months before COVID.
- **COVID → drag racing** full time, to championship level (he was set to run Nitro
  in 2026 before pivoting).
- **Early 2026 — the pivot.** The Skydio drone convinced him autonomy had truly
  arrived; Claude made real engineering work possible. He turned the whole stack —
  power, fiber, thermal, structure, AI — toward **ADC Solutions Inc**.

## Built and shipped (the proof)

Scott is a *maker*, not an integrator. The receipts:

**Production race hardware — under the CP-Carrillo name.** Scott **designed the
Big Bore Pistons and race connecting rods for the Harley-Davidson Milwaukee-8
(2017–present)**, produced through **CP-Carrillo** — a top-tier piston and rod
manufacturer that does not build hobbyist parts. Sold through his own brand,
**STFU Motorcycles** ("Scott's Tuning & Factory Upgraded Motorcycles,"
[stfumotorcycles.com](https://stfumotorcycles.com)) — a full shop with a
**Dynojet 250i dyno, SuperFlow 600 flowbench, and in-house machine shop**.

**Inventions he still sells — and gives away.** The **Rocker Box Buddy** (2018)
and the **SE Air Cleaner Modification Kit** (built around an S&B-manufactured
filter, fits 2017–2023 Screamin' Eagle oval air cleaners). Scott **deliberately
patents neither** — he calls them open source and isn't worried about it. That's
a genuine value: he favors open over locked-down, and it's the same instinct
behind the next item.

**AI-native before the wave — "AI Advantage" and Roxy.** In **February 2026**,
months before most of his industry could spell "agent," Scott built **Roxy**, an
AI voice agent, and formed a company around her called **AI Advantage**. Roxy
runs on **Qwen3-8B** (open weights) with **18 tools baked in** and answers in a
British accent. **The demo line is still live — call 337-486-3149 and talk to
her.**

**Autonomous operations — today.** Scott doesn't *use* an AI assistant; he runs
ADC Solutions through an **autonomous AI "Mission Control"** with the company's
GitHub repository as its single source of truth. He builds and operates AI the
same way he builds and operates everything else.

- **Marine and recovery** — ROV piloting/supervision and dive capability directly
  applicable to droneship support and booster/hardware recovery.
- **Tune the robots** — as embodied AI floods the physical world, Scott is the
  rare operator who understands the *model* and masters the *machine it lives in*.

No MIT pedigree — by design. An engineer can train a model; **Scott can land it
in the mud, power it, cool it, rig it with a crane, fix it when it breaks, and
tune it to run** — offshore or onshore. Not credentialed in it. **Proven in it.**
