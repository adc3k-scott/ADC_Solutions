## §1 Purpose & Scope

One procurement package for **one reference cluster** — everything Scott can hand to suppliers to source materials, in two discipline families:

- **E-bundles (electrical, new scope):** white-space power distribution from the RPP to the rack outlet — RPPs, 600 A busway, tap-off boxes, whips, grounding.
- **M-bundles (mechanical, roll-up):** cluster-level quantities that point at the already-issued packages (CDU BOM, TCS BoD bundles B1–B9, HAC kit) — referenced, never restated.

**Scope boundary (decided 2026-06-11):** electrical starts at the **RPP line lugs**. Everything upstream (transformer, switchgear, the microgrid node) is MGN-BOD-002 / site scope. Rack PDUs and IT are owner scope; whips (E4) are the optional last meter.

## §3 Electrical Architecture — straight from Redmond §18.7/§18.8

The Deschutes/Redmond HAC interface **defines** the busway scheme; ADC adopts it unmodified for the first sourcing run:

- **8 × 600 A busducts per row — 4 per side of the hot aisle** (placement fungible, carried by the HAC cross-aisle beams). [L] — Deschutes §18.7
- **"4 makes 3":** every rack spot takes 4 feeds (one per busduct on its side), 3+1 redundant. [L] — Deschutes §18.8
- **System voltage: three-phase 230/240 Vac Wye** (= 400–415 V line-to-line). Matches the CDU's 380–416 Vac dual-feed PDU. [L] — Table 18.8.1; Deschutes §3.1
- **Outlets: IEC 60309 5-pin (3P+N+E) 30/32 A, two outlets per rack spot per feed**, each outlet behind 32 A 3-phase tap-box protection. Per-rack-spot swap to single-phase tap boxes for legacy racks. [L] — Table 18.8.1
- **Upstream redundancy rule:** UPS and generator redundancy each ≥ feed redundancy. Source topology upstream of the RPPs is ELE-OI-02. [O]
- **Diablo 400 (±400 Vdc / 800 V sidecar) remains the roadmap** for high-density follow-ons; nothing in this package precludes it. [W] — Scott decision 2026-06-11

### §3.1 Load check at 78 kW avg (all comfortable)

| Point | Normal (4 feeds) | Failover (3 of 4) | Rating | Margin check |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Outlet (8/rack) | 9.75 kW ≈ 14 A | 13 kW ≈ 18.8 A | 32 A ckt | 59% of rating at failover ✓ |
| Busduct (8 racks/side served) | 156 kW ≈ 217 A | 208 kW ≈ 289 A | 600 A | 48% ✓ (56% even at the 90 kW spot envelope) |
| RPP source (2 busducts each) | 312 kW ≈ 434 A | 416 kW ≈ 578 A | 800 A frame [W] | 72% ✓ |

Currents at 415 V 3φ. The 600 A rating is the OCP-specified duct, not a sizing choice — at 78 kW it runs half-loaded, which is the headroom that makes the 90 kW spot envelope and later density bumps free.

### §3.2 Topology

**4 RPPs (sources A/B/C/D)**, each feeding **2 busducts — one per side of the aisle** — so losing any one source removes exactly one feed from every rack (4-makes-3 preserved). Per RPP: 2 × 600 A feeder breakers + spares; the CDU's two cords land as 150 A 3-pole breakers in RPP-A and RPP-B (CDU ≈ 74 kW, 103 A FLA — 125 A frame violates the 80% rule at 82%, hence 150 A). One 100 A auxiliary panelboard (controls, leak-detection electronics, HAC lighting) off RPP-C. [W]

## §5 What E-bundles deliberately exclude

- Feeder conductors RPP→busway end feeds, conduit, installation labor — install contractor scope, quoted separately.
- Cable tray / wire baskets — already in the **HAC kit** (Levels 2–4); not double-bought here.
- Rack PDUs — owner/IT scope (Table 18.8.1 outlet is the demarcation).
- UPS / generation — MGN-BOD-002 and site scope (ELE-OI-02 carries the redundancy-matching rule).
